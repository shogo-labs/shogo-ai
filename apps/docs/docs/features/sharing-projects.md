# Sharing Projects

Shogo gives you control over who can see and interact with your projects. You can keep projects private, share them with your team, or make them accessible to anyone.

## Project visibility

Every project has a visibility setting that controls who can access it within the workspace:

- **Private** — Only you can see the project
- **Workspace** — All members of your workspace can see and access the project

:::note
Project visibility controls access to the project editor and source code. It's separate from [access control for published apps](./access-control), which controls who can visit the live URL.
:::

## Sharing with your team

When a project's visibility is set to **Workspace**, all members can:

- View the project on the dashboard
- Open the project editor
- Send messages in the chat
- View the preview, code, and history

To change project visibility:

1. Open the project.
2. Go to **Project Settings** (gear icon).
3. Under **Visibility**, choose your preferred setting.

## Starring projects

You can star your favorite projects for quick access:

1. Find the project on your dashboard.
2. Click the **Star** icon on the project card.
3. Access all starred projects from the **Starred** section in the sidebar.

Starring is personal — it doesn't affect other workspace members.

## Finding projects

Your dashboard provides several ways to find projects:

- **All Projects** — Browse everything in your workspace
- **Starred** — Your favorited projects
- **Shared** — Projects shared with you
- **Search** — Use the search bar to find projects by name
- **Folders** — Organize projects into folders for better organization

## Organizing with folders

Keep your workspace tidy by grouping related projects into folders:

1. Go to your dashboard.
2. Create a new folder.
3. Drag projects into folders, or move them via the project menu.

Folders are visible to all workspace members.

## FAQ

**Can people outside my workspace see my projects?**
No. Project access is limited to workspace members (unless the project is published — see [Publishing](./publishing)).

**Can I prevent specific members from seeing a project?**
Currently, project visibility is either private (only you) or workspace-wide (all members). Fine-grained permissions per member are not yet available.

**Does sharing affect my published app?**
No. Project sharing and published app access are independent settings. You can share a project with your team for editing while keeping the published app private, or vice versa.
